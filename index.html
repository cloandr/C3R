Prothesis Set Up is Challenging

For orthoprosthetists, the adjustment of a tibial prosthesis remains a challenging task, often extending over several months. The primary objective of this fitting process is to align the prosthesis with the patient's leg. Achieving the appropriate prosthesis-leg alignment requires locating the center of gravity. An improperly adjusted prosthesis can impede the patient's gait and walking ability.

Eric Doe an orthoprothesist looked for a tool to optimize the setup and tightening of tibial prothesis and reduce the lack of errors for an improved stability. He came to us to design a setting device for tibial prothesis.

This project consists of the design of a connected device integrated into a tibial prosthesis and linked to a mobile application via Bluetooth. Intended for patients who have lost a lower limb, this device is used to recover and visualize the patient's center of gravity. This data is essential to ensure precise adjustment of the prosthesis adapted to the patient.

Our Clients' Needs

Technical Expectations

  • Collect position data from patients center of gravity data to make prothesis settings
  • Visualize gravity center on the mobile application
  • Minimize clutter space between wearable setting system and the prothesis
  • Develop an easy-to-use mobile app for professionals

Our Technology

We aim to make this process easier. We develop a wearable setting system to be integrated in your tibial prosthesis, where adjustments are vizualized in your phone. Prothesis system sends gravity center position in real time condition to ensure the prothesis is correctly aligned to provide better comfort and balance. It empowers healtcare professionals to correctly place the prothesis. Designed for patients who have experienced lower limb loss, this device serves the purpose of capturing and displaying the patient's center of gravity. These data are crucial in ensuring a precise and tailored adjustment of the prosthesis to meet the patient's needs.
